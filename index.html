I'm a doctoral student in Computer Science at the School of Interactive Computing at Georgia Institute of Technology. I am a member of the Social Dynamics and Wellbeing lab and I am advised by Munmun De Choudhury.

My research interests are in Computational Social Science (Social Computing) wherein I am interested in studying human behavior as manifested on online social media platforms. My research explores statistical machine learning, natural language processing and social network analysis methods to analyze large scale social media data to derive insights specific to behavioral changes, specifically in relation to health and wellbeing. In one line of research, I am studying the phenomenon of online self disclosure, via behavioral changes (linguistic, network and interactional) on social media. In another line of research, I am developing computational methods that employ social media data of a clinical population to quantify and predict risk to mental disorders (schizophrenia) and forecast the illness course.

Prior to Tech, I was at International Institute of Information Technology Hyderabad, India where I completed my undergraduate and graduate studies (majoring in Computer Science with a research specialization in Computational Humanities). A lot of my work from back then was around user modeling, personality mining and ontology engineering. Outside academia (is there really an outside?), I am a Grade 3 violinist and I love cooking and traveling.
