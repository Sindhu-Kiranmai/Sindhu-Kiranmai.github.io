I am a first year PhD student at the School of Interactive Computing at Georgia Insitute of Technology. I am interested in interdisciplinary, collaborative research at the intersection of Social Science and Computing. My research interests primarily lie in Social Computing (Computational Social Science). A lot of my work focuses on using Data Analytics, Natural Language Processing and Machine Learning methods to analyze large scale social data available on the web. I am currently part of the Social Dynamics and Wellbeing lab at Georgia Tech, where I am advised by Prof. Munmun De Choudhury. At present, we are working on examining behavioral and linguistic markers leading to social media disclosures on stigmatized mental health concerns such as Schizophrenia to characterize wellbeing.
